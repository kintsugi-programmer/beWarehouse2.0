
TelemetryTradeAI – The AI-powered Bloomberg for Retail Traders

Definition
TelemetryTradeAI is an AI-powered unified intelligence platform designed as the Bloomberg equivalent for retail traders in India. It solves the broken trader workflow by combining real-time multi-asset charting, verified Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) research, and portfolio analysis from screenshots into one seamless product. Unlike a simple chatbot, it follows the three-layer architecture to take market data and user input, reason over it, and deliver actionable, cited outputs.


Importance
This product demonstrates why AI products are different — it moves from fragmented tools and hallucination-prone chatbots to a single, trustworthy agentic system that saves hours of research, reduces monetary risk, and creates a predictive customer journey for India’s 192 million demat account holders. It directly applies Human-Machine Interaction, Responsible AI, and multimodal design principles taught in the course.


Example 1
For an active trader (age 22–35, tech-savvy), the platform provides real-time multi-asset charting (stocks, crypto, forex) with INR/USD dual-currency overlay using TradingView and CoinGecko feeds. When the user asks a research question, the Verified RAG system pulls from whitepapers and 10-K filings and gives every answer with clickable citations, eliminating hallucinations and enabling confident decisions in minutes instead of hours.


Example 2
For a small fund or quant user, the Portfolio AI feature allows uploading any broker app screenshot. The OCR engine reads and structures the portfolio data, while the AI agent delivers a risk score, sector exposure, asset distribution, and diversification tips. This turns a fragmented workflow into one unified, actionable experience.


AI Architecture
TelemetryTradeAI follows the exact three-layer architecture. The Model and Compute layer uses Gemini API for LLM-based RAG research and portfolio analysis, CoinGecko API for live market data, and TradingView Widget for professional charting. The Agent and Orchestration layer includes the RAG pipeline (retrieves verified documents and rejects hallucinations), OCR ingestion for screenshots, multi-asset engine, and query router. The App and Product layer is built in Next.js 14 with a streaming AI chat UI, token discovery dashboard, and portfolio analyser that delivers the final user experience with inline citations.


Product Future with AI
The future roadmap includes anomaly detection, explainable AI (XAI) confidence scores, strategy backtesting, PDF research reports, news signal aggregator, and community features. Eventually it will evolve into a fully agentic personal trading co-pilot that autonomously monitors portfolios and executes alerts or suggestions with user approval.



AI Laws and Security
As a finance application, it must comply with SEBI/RBI guidelines, DPDP Act 2023 for data consent, and February 2026 India AI Governance Guidelines for transparency and human oversight. The Verified RAG pipeline and cited answers directly address Responsible AI requirements and reduce security risks from hallucinations or misinformation.

Limitations
Even with Verified RAG, the system still depends on the quality of ingested documents and can face latency in real-time market data. OCR accuracy may vary with poor screenshot quality, and building a competitive moat against big players requires continuous updates to the RAG pipeline and multi-asset engine.

Why are AI Products Different? 

Definition
An AI-based application  is a system that takes input, reasons over it, and decides the best course of action to produce an output that helps achieve a goal. To understand these systems, we break them into three layers: Model and Compute at the bottom, Agent and Orchestration in the middle, and App and Product at the top.


Importance
AI products are different from traditional software because they are probabilistic, adaptive, and continuously learning rather than following fixed rules. This shift requires designers to move from one-time build-and-deploy thinking to lifecycle management with constant feedback loops. It enables hyper-personalisation, predictive experiences, and new forms of value creation, but also introduces unique challenges of trust, explainability, and harm prevention.


Example 1
Razorpay’s Voice Agentic Payments is a clear example. The user speaks naturally in any Indian language, the AI reasons about the intent, orchestrates the payment flow through the Model Context Protocol, and completes the transaction securely. The entire journey happens in one voice conversation without any manual steps.


Example 2
Granola AI is another strong example. During a meeting the user types rough notes while the AI captures system audio locally, reasons over the transcript, and enhances the notes into structured summaries and action items while keeping the original user text in black and AI suggestions in grey. This gives the user full control and creates a collaborative experience.


AI Architecture
The architecture consists of three distinct layers. The lowest layer is Model and Compute that provides the data, algorithms, and raw intelligence. The middle layer is Agent and Orchestration that handles reasoning, planning, memory, and tool usage. The top layer is the App and Product layer that presents the final interface and closes the feedback loop with the user.


Product Future with AI
By 2028–2030, most digital products will become fully agentic. They will autonomously achieve complex goals, work as teammates rather than tools, and operate across multiple modalities while maintaining continuous learning and adaptation.


AI Laws and Security
These products must comply with India’s DPDP Act 2023, the February 2026 India AI Governance Guidelines, and the IT Rules amendment for labelling AI-generated content. Security concerns include prompt injection, model poisoning, and data leakage, which require adversarial training and strong output guardrails.


Limitations
The main limitations are non-deterministic behaviour that can lead to hallucinations, high compute cost, black-box explainability issues, and potential safety and alignment challenges when agents act autonomously.
